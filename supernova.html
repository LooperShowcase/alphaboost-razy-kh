Supernova

Compared to a star's entire history, the visual appearance of a supernova is very brief, sometimes spanning several months,
so that the chances of observing one with the naked eye is roughly once in a lifetime.
Only a tiny fraction of the 100 billion stars in a typical galaxy have the capacity to become a supernova,
being restricted to those having high mass and rare kinds of binary stars containing white dwarfs.
